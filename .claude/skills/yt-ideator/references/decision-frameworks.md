## 1. TAM Scoring Framework

Rate every idea on a 1-5 TAM scale. This replaces the vague "does it have broad appeal?" with a concrete assessment.

**TAM 5 — Universal:** Almost anyone could click this. Not niche-dependent. Think MrBeast territory.
- Example: "Ages 1 to 100 Fight for $500,000"
- Audience: Hundreds of millions. Language/culture doesn't matter much.

**TAM 4 — Broad niche:** Anyone with a passing interest in the topic area. Doesn't require being "in" the niche.
- Example: "I Built My Best Body With Only 10-Minute Workouts" (anyone who's ever thought about fitness)
- Audience: Tens of millions. Reaches beyond subscribers.

**TAM 3 — Core niche:** Targets people actively engaged in the niche. Your subscriber base + people searching for this topic.
- Example: "How to Edit a YouTube Video" (only people making YouTube videos)
- Audience: Millions. Solid performer but won't go mega-viral.

**TAM 2 — Sub-niche:** Targets a specific segment within the niche. Useful for authority-building but limited reach.
- Example: "Best OBS Settings for Streaming in 2026"
- Audience: Hundreds of thousands. Good for SEO, not for breakout.

**TAM 1 — Micro-niche:** Very specific audience. Only useful if that audience is extremely high-value.
- Example: "How to Color Grade Sony A7IV Footage in DaVinci Resolve"
- Audience: Tens of thousands. Skip unless building a very targeted business.

**Rule of thumb:** For breakout videos, aim for TAM 4+. For consistent content, TAM 3 is fine. Avoid TAM 1-2 unless strategically building authority in a specific area.

**Channel size adjustments:**
- Under 10K subs: You NEED TAM 4-5 ideas to break through. Your existing audience can't carry a video — the idea has to pull strangers in.
- 10K-100K subs: Mix of TAM 3-4. Your audience gives you a floor, but you still need broader ideas to grow.
- 100K+ subs: TAM 3 is fine for most videos. Your audience is big enough to make niche ideas work. Sprinkle in TAM 4-5 for growth spurts.

## 2. Feasibility Checklist

For each idea, quickly assess production requirements. Rate as Low / Medium / High effort.

### Production Complexity

**Low effort (1-2 days):**
- Talking head + graphics/B-roll
- Screen recording + voiceover
- Solo filming in existing setup
- Commentary on existing content
- List/ranking format with research

**Medium effort (3-7 days):**
- Requires specific location or setup
- Needs 1-2 guests or collaborations
- Multi-day filming (transformation, challenge)
- Requires props, equipment, or special materials
- Needs custom graphics or animations

**High effort (1-4 weeks):**
- Multi-location shoot
- Requires travel
- 30-day challenge or long-term documentation
- Needs large group of participants
- Professional production quality required
- Requires permissions, licenses, or access

### Quick Decision Matrix

Ask these three questions:
1. **Can they film this in their current setup?** (Yes = low effort bump)
2. **Does it require other people?** (No = easier, Yes = plan ahead)
3. **Does it require time to pass?** (30-day challenge = high effort, one-day shoot = low)

When presenting ideas, always note the effort level so the creator can plan their timeline and production accordingly.

## 3. Evidence Standards

When claiming an idea "has evidence," cite specific proof. Here's what counts:

### Strong Evidence (high confidence)
- A comparable video with 5x+ the channel's average views
- Multiple videos on similar topics across different channels all performing well
- Trending topic with documented search volume growth
- Outlier data from tools (1of10.com, vidIQ, etc.) showing clear overperformance

### Moderate Evidence (good direction)
- Similar concept worked in an adjacent niche (different audience, same format)
- One strong example from a comparable channel
- The topic aligns with known audience desires (from viewer searches, comments)
- Cultural trend or news cycle creating temporary demand

### Weak Evidence (gut feeling)
- "This feels like it would work" — not sufficient alone
- One old example (2+ years ago) — audience behavior has changed
- Only works if the creator has a specific skillset/personality
- Based on a trend that may have peaked

**Standard when presenting:** Always cite at least one specific example per idea. If you can't find evidence, flag it: "This is a gut-call — no direct evidence, but the underlying desire is validated by [X]."

## 4. Niche Core Desires

Before ideating for any niche, identify the audience's core fantasy. This drives everything — titles, thumbnails, emotional hooks.

**YouTube Growth:** "I want more views and subscribers. I want to quit my job and do YouTube full-time."
**Business/Entrepreneurship:** "I want financial freedom. I want to escape the 9-5 and be my own boss."
**Fitness/Health:** "I want to look better and feel confident. I want transformation with minimal time."
**Personal Finance:** "I want to be rich. I want to stop worrying about money."
**Productivity:** "I want more time. I want to get more done without burning out."
**Tech/Gadgets:** "I want the best stuff. I want to make smart purchase decisions."
**Gaming:** "I want to be better at this game. I want entertainment and community."
**Beauty/Fashion:** "I want to look amazing. I want confidence and admiration."
**Education/Study:** "I want to be smarter, faster. I want to succeed without suffering."
**Travel:** "I want adventure and escape. I want to see the world."
**Food/Cooking:** "I want to make amazing food easily. I want to impress people."
**Relationships/Dating:** "I want to be loved and desired. I want to understand people."

For any niche not listed: Ask "what does this audience daydream about?" The answer is your north star for every thumbnail concept and title hook.

## 5. Complementary Color Quick Reference

**Highest-impact combos (proven by top creators):**

| Combo | Emotion/Feel | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Orange + Blue | Energy, trust, contrast | Most versatile — works for everything |
| Yellow + Purple | Luxury, excitement, royalty | Business, entertainment, premium content |
| Red + Cyan/Teal | Urgency, freshness | Challenges, reactions, time-sensitive |
| Pink + Green | Playful, natural | Beauty, health, lifestyle |
| Red + Blue | Power, competition | Sports, gaming, versus content |

**Color → Emotion shortcuts:**
- Red: urgency, danger, passion, anger
- Orange: energy, enthusiasm, warmth
- Yellow: optimism, attention, caution
- Green: growth, money, health, nature
- Blue: trust, calm, professionalism
- Purple: luxury, mystery, creativity
- Pink: playful, feminine, love
- Black: power, sophistication, drama
- White: clean, simple, pure

**Rule:** The background/environment should be one color, the subject/focal point should be the complement. This creates natural visual separation that stops the scroll.

## 6. Sequencing Strategy

When presenting multiple concepts, recommend a publication order:

### Breakout Sequence (for channels trying to grow)
1. **Lead with highest TAM** — Cast the widest net to pull in new viewers
2. **Follow with "proof" content** — Transformation, results, or challenge that builds credibility
3. **Then go deeper** — More niche concepts that convert new viewers into loyal subscribers
4. **Repeat cycle** — Broad → Prove → Deepen → Broad

### Momentum Rules
- Don't stack two similar formats back-to-back (e.g., two ranking videos in a row)
- After a breakout video, publish within 3-5 days to capture the algorithm boost
- Alternate between "growth" videos (high TAM) and "depth" videos (builds loyalty)
- If a video underperforms, don't panic — follow it with a proven-format idea, not another experiment

### Spacing
- Big production videos: 2-3 weeks apart (need prep time)
- Standard videos: Weekly
- Trend-based ideas: Publish ASAP — trends decay fast
