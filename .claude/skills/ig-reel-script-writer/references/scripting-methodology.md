# Viral Scripting Methodology

## The 5-Part Script Structure

Every reel script follows this structure: **Hook → Build-Up → Value → Payoff → CTA**

---

### 1. Hook (0-3 seconds)

The hook IS the entire video concept. It is the single most important factor determining a video's success.

**Purpose:** Generate enough curiosity or interest that it's psychologically impossible for the viewer to scroll past without watching.

**Rules:**
- Write ONE sentence that encapsulates the video concept — the rest of the script flows from this
- Make the viewer crave the resolution/payoff to the premise
- Be subtle — don't shout, don't be flashy. A good concept said in a normal speaking voice will hook people
- NEVER give away the payoff in the hook — if they already know the answer, they scroll
- For virality, the hook needs MASS APPEAL (broad audience, not hyper-niche)
- For conversions, hooks can be more specific and tactical

**Weak hooks = predictable.** If the viewer can guess the payoff from the hook alone, they scroll. Strong hooks create genuine uncertainty about what the resolution will be.

**Weak hook patterns to avoid:**
- Too specific/no mass appeal: "If you're not using this one tool to source profitable products for your online arbitrage business..."
- Bland/generic: "This is how you're gonna use your phone to make an extra $2k per month"
- Giving away the payoff: "You're not gonna solve your problems by avoiding the actual work" (this is the lesson, not the hook)
- Predictable biz-op pitch: "This side hustle made me $10,000 in the last 6 months"

**Strong hook transformation:**
- WEAK: "This side hustle made me $10,000 in the last 6 months"
- STRONG: "Testing side hustles part 1 — everyone says I can make $1000s a day dropshipping, so let's see if that's true!"

The strong version creates genuine uncertainty (will it work or not?) and uses a relatable "testing" frame instead of preaching.

---

### 2. Build-Up (3-10 seconds)

After the hook, increase the viewer's commitment to watching. Make them CRAVE the resolution even more before delivering value.

**Purpose:** Build suspense, hype up the upcoming payoff, and clear objections so they stay watching.

**Techniques:**

**Mind Read** — Anticipate what the viewer assumes and negate it. This clears their predicted payoff so they MUST keep watching.
- "And NO, I didn't..."
- "And before you say anything, NO it's not ______ or ______"

**Risk Reversal** — Remove any perceived barriers so they feel they have nothing to lose.
- "And the crazy thing is, this will only take a minute to set up"
- "Even if you suck at ______, this will work for you, I promise"
- "I'm going to put a lot of power in your hands, so use this at your own discretion"
- "Watch me do it in under a minute"

**Authority Endorsement** — Back the hook with credibility from known figures or results.
- "This is the same way [Company or Person] [Desired Outcome]"
- "This is the same secret sauce that people like [Authority 1], [Authority 2] are using right now"

**Controversy** — Validate or challenge an opinion to get an emotional response.
- "And I know what I'm about to say might make everyone upset"
- "Don't get me wrong, [common approach] is good, but [your approach] is just so much better"

**Personal Authority** — Use your own social proof.
- "And this is the same thing I've done to reach [Desired Outcome]"
- "And this is how you can learn from my mistake so it doesn't happen to you"

---

### 3. Value (10-45 seconds, varies by length)

This is the "sauce" — specific, actionable information the viewer can immediately benefit from.

**Rules:**
- Must be SPECIFIC and ACTIONABLE — tips, steps, tools, websites, resources, insights
- Give away stuff that ONLY YOU know but EVERYONE could benefit from
- CUT OUT ALL FLUFF — every single line must serve a purpose
- Litmus test: "Would the viewer pay $5 for this information?"
- Give away your best stuff — paid-level value for free. This is how you stand out
- The more extreme the value, the better the video performs AND converts

**Extreme value examples:**
- Showing exact settings/formats (viewers pause and screenshot)
- Offering to connect them with your team
- Giving away a paid strategy for free
- Providing dollar amounts of free ad spend or resources

**The gatekeeping trap:** Most creators are too scared to give away their real sauce. That's why their videos underperform. Give away paid-level value freely — the law of reciprocity will convert viewers into followers and customers.

---

### 4. Payoff

The moment the viewer gets the resolution to the hook. Hook and payoff work as a pair.

**Rules:**
- NEVER give the payoff right away — hold off as long as possible
- Build suspense before the payoff
- Once you deliver the payoff, retention drops — they got what they came for
- Everything between hook and payoff is what drives watch time

---

### 5. CTA (last 2-5 seconds)

Leverage the law of reciprocity — you just delivered massive value, now ask for a small favor.

**Best CTAs (ranked):**
1. **Keep giving** (strongest): "Comment [WORD] and I'll send you [free resource]" — reciprocity kicks in AND you generate leads
2. **Comment CTA**: "Comment ______ for ______"
3. **Share CTA**: "Share this with a friend who _____"
4. **Save CTA**: "Save this video so you don't forget next time you ____"
5. **Action CTA**: "Go out and USE the information I just gave you"
6. **Link CTA**: "Hit the link in my bio" (honestly not bad)
7. **Follow CTA**: "Follow for more value" (weakest — sounds like begging)

**BONUS:** CTAs that keep giving instead of asking outperform everything. "If you want even more [value], just comment [WORD] and I'll send it to you" crushes "please follow me."

## The 6 Core Principles of Persuasion

Weave these throughout the script subtly (never heavy-handedly):

1. **Authority** — Show credibility through proof, setting, results (not bragging)
2. **Social Proof** — Show others are benefiting or watching
3. **Liking** — Be relatable, authentic, entertaining
4. **Commitment/Consistency** — Assign an identity the viewer wants to embody
5. **Reciprocity** — Give insane value, they feel obligated to follow/engage
6. **Scarcity/Urgency** — Limited spots, limited time, rare info

---

## Psychology Tricks Toolkit

Use these throughout the script as needed:

- **Burning Question** — Create an unanswered question they must resolve
- **Controversy** — Challenge a belief or validate a hot take
- **Pain Points** — Hit a pain the viewer is experiencing, promise the solution
- **Authority Hijacking** — Borrow credibility from known names/brands
- **Trending Topics** — Ride a cultural moment
- **Desired Outcome** — Paint the dream life/result the viewer craves
- **Hype** — Build excitement and anticipation for the value
- **Secret Info** — Position your value as insider knowledge
- **Funny/Foul/Slang Words** — Casual language that feels authentic
- **Risk Reversal** — Remove all perceived barriers
- **Mind Read** — Anticipate and negate assumptions
- **Unexpected Twist** — Flip expectations to reset curiosity
- **Actionable Value** — Steps they can take RIGHT NOW
- **Extreme Value** — Give away paid-level stuff for free
- **Payoff** — The resolution to the hook's promise
- **Comment CTAs** — Drive engagement with specific comment prompts

---

## Key Principles

1. **Subtlety wins.** Psychology tricks should be invisible to the viewer. Don't be flashy, don't shout, don't brag. Show, don't tell.
2. **Authority through setting, not words.** Show your lifestyle/results naturally (home office, setup) rather than saying "trust me, I'm rich."
3. **"Day in the life" > "How to."** Relatable framing outperforms preachy teaching. Show, don't lecture.
4. **Every line must serve a purpose.** If a line isn't annotated with a psychology trick or structural purpose, cut it.
5. **Hook determines success.** Spend the most time crafting the hook. If the hook is good, the rest writes itself — just riff on the concept.
6. **Give away your best stuff.** The law of reciprocity converts better than any sales pitch.
