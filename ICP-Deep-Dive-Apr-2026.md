Built from Albert Wang (2026-03-12), Cole Baker (2026-04-02), Conaugh "Kana" McKenna (2026-04-17). Every quote is verbatim from one of those calls.

## The One-Sentence ICP

A 23–32 year-old technical operator doing $10–40k/mo through outreach, ads, or a small paid community — who has already built his own AI content stack, knows the strategy cold, and still can't crack organic views or build a personal brand he's proud of.

He doesn't need a strategy. He needs the execution layer he can't see — and an identity shift that lets him stop "figuring it out myself."

---

## Composite Profile

**Who he is:**
- Male, 22–32, North America
- Technical / operator / builder mindset. Calls himself "scrappy," "stubborn," "a technical dude," "AI-native"
- Already runs a real business: paid community ($300+ MRR), ads agency ($27k/mo), AI implementation agency ($10k MRR + $30–40k project work)
- Already a "guru wall" graduate — has paid for multiple programs (Hormozi AI, Bennett Spooner, trading-program coaches)
- Has built his own homemade AI content pipeline using Claude Projects, Gemini, Yorbi, custom Python, RAG, etc.
- Studies competitors obsessively. Copies their formats. Knows the frameworks.
- Has somewhere between 0 and ~50k followers. Often verified. Sparse profile.

**What he sells:**
- Trading / investing communities (Albert)
- Paid-ad services + retainers (Cole)
- AI implementation builds for "old heads" — real estate, manufacturing, retail (Kana)

**What's working:**
- Back-end (retention, fulfillment, conversion, offer)
- Closing on referrals or warm intros
- Building cool tech / running paid traffic / running a community

**What's broken:**
- Top of funnel
- Personal brand
- Consistent organic distribution
- Scripting in his own voice
- Showing up on camera in a way he doesn't hate

## The Five Core Pains (Ranked By Universality)

### 1. The Execution Gap (#1 pain — all three voiced this)
> "I know what works. But for some reason, it's like not working as well for me." — Albert

He has the strategy. Has the knowledge. Has the AI tools. Has watched the breakdowns. **But the same format that gets the 5k follower guy 300k views gets him 200.** He doesn't know what subtle thing he's missing — tonality, visual hook, energy, framing — and AI can't tell him because AI doesn't know either.

The pain isn't "I don't know what to do." The pain is "I'm doing it and it still isn't working and I can't see why."

### 2. The Identity Crisis
> "Am I a DJ? Am I an AI builder? Am I just a regular brand influencer?" — Kana

He's not a creator. He's an operator who has to become a creator to grow. He doesn't know what his "brand" is, what voice to use, what to talk about. He has too many things going on and no clear positioning. Every time he sits down to film he stares at the camera and doesn't know who he's talking as.

### 3. Failing In Public
> "With entrepreneurism, you fail behind closed doors. With content, I have to fail in front of people. It messes with my head." — Kana

This is the hidden objection no one talks about. He has revenue, status, peers. Posting bad content where it can be seen by his network feels like risk. So he doesn't post. Or he posts low-effort. Or he posts and deletes.

### 4. Quality / Output Anxiety
> "My content is shit. It's terrible." — Cole
> "I want higher level output. I would want the systems for that." — Cole
> "Sometimes it's still spitting out hooks that are pretty mid." — Albert

He's used to shipping good work in his real business. The bar is high. So when he sees his own talking head — bad lighting, mid hook, no editing — it physically hurts. The gap between his standards and his output is too big to close alone.

### 5. Time / Capacity / Burnout
> "I'm working harder than I was before. People think I'm sitting on a beach drinking a margarita." — Kana
> "I'm 80% capacity and worried quality will drop." — Cole
> "I'm in a program right now and it's not working." — Albert

He's already maxed. He doesn't have 4 hours a day to learn content. He needs leverage. He needs DFY. He needs systems that compress the time-to-quality.

## The Three Identity Tensions To Exploit In Content

These are the internal contradictions he hasn't reconciled. Calling them out by name is the highest-conviction content lever you have.

**1. He's scrappy AND he's stuck.** He prides himself on "figuring it out himself." He's also been stuck for 30 posts in 200-view jail. The scrappy identity is now the ceiling. (Hook: "Your scrappiness is the reason you're stuck.")

**2. He's technical AND he can't crack the human layer.** He built a system that scrapes his videos, compares them to competitors, audits his hooks. It still doesn't work. (Hook: "You can't engineer your way out of bad tonality.")

**3. He has revenue AND a brand he's embarrassed by.** He can charge $25k retainers but his Instagram looks like nothing. (Hook: "Your back end is dialed. Your top of funnel looks like a 19-year-old's burner.")

## The Real "Why Now" Triggers (Watch For These In DMs)

A prospect lights up on the first call when one or more of these is fresh:
- Just hit 80%+ capacity in their current business
- Just lost a major lead source (ad account banned, cold email open rates dropped, key referrer churned)
- Just watched a peer with worse fundamentals blow up on Instagram
- Just spent $X on a coaching program that didn't deliver views
- Just built their own AI content stack and watched it produce mid output
- Just realized their best client could leave tomorrow and there's no replacement pipeline

When you're writing TOF content, name these moments. They will see themselves.

## What NOT To Do In Content (Because These Three Will Tune Out Instantly)

- Don't talk down. They are smarter than 90% of the creator-coach audience.
- Don't pitch "AI content" as a feature. They've already built it. The wedge is: AI + the human layer they can't engineer.
- Don't position as "coach." Position as operator / partner / one-step-ahead peer. (Kana literally rejected the coach dynamic on the call.)
- Don't use "guru wall" aesthetics. Cole specifically called these out. No mansions, no Lambos, no fake whiteboards.
- Don't promise "viral." Albert pushed back: "I'm not really wanting to do it for virality." Promise the unlock — views that compound, brand that converts.
- Don't use beginner pain points (no website, no offer, no audience). They have all of that. Speak from inside the operator world.
- Don't use generic creator language ("be authentic, post daily"). Speak in their operator vocabulary — funnel, pipeline, capacity, leverage, unlock.

## The Conversion Path This Content Needs To Build

**Stage 1 — Recognition.** TOF post names a pain in their words. They stop scrolling. ("That's literally me.")

**Stage 2 — Reframe.** MOF post installs the new belief. Shows the mechanism. ("Oh. AI can't fix this. The execution layer is human. I've been wasting reps.")

**Stage 3 — Authority.** Case-study post or BTS post. Shows the result a real operator got from working with you. ("OK, this guy gets it. He's done what I want to do, with a client like me.")

**Stage 4 — Offer.** BOF post or DM trigger. Names the offer, the guarantee, the scarcity, the ICP fit. ("I should book a call.")

**Stage 5 — Call.** They show up to the call already pre-sold on the mechanism. Your job on the call is identity-fit and trust, not pitching.
