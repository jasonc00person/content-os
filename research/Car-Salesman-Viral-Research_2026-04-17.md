## Patterns to Steal

- **Cold-open sensory hook.** Raffi opens reels mid-scene with something audibly or visually weird ("where is that sound?") — no intro, no context. Forces the viewer to stay for the payoff.
- **Serialized mockumentary.** Mohawk Chevrolet built a multi-episode "The Dealership" series. Recurring characters + running jokes = compounding retention across posts.
- **Expose as Trojan horse.** George Saliba and Delivrd both use "here's the shady trick" framing to funnel into paid offers. Audience feels like they're getting insider info, not a pitch.
- **One unforgettable trait beats clever scripts.** The opera-singing salesman went viral on a single gimmick. Character > copy.
- **Niche depth scales further than you'd think.** Doug Horner (173K) and Russ (473K) built real businesses on sub-500K followings by owning narrow lanes (luxury insider, dealership trainer). Niche depth > broad reach for high-ticket conversion.
