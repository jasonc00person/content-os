# Higgsfield Campaign — 10 Viral Reel Ideas

Pulled directly from the brief's hooks, FOMO lines, main statement, feature walkthrough, and CTA direction. Each idea uses a different combo of the brief's elements to create a distinct reel.

**Brief requirements for all:** Demo all 5 features on higgsfield.ai with your own generations, tag @higgsfield.ai, include "Higgsfield" + #HiggsfieldCreator in caption, CTA to Higgsfield link in bio or comment automation.

---

## 1. "The Gold Rush" — Straight Main Statement Delivery

**Brief element:** Main Statement + Hook Option #1
**Hook:** "We're in the middle of the biggest gold rush in our generation and 99% of people aren't noticing it."
**Structure:** Deliver the main statement right after the hook — "There's a gold rush happening in the creator economy right now. Most creators don't see it. The ones who do? They're producing studio-level content faster, cheaper, and at a scale you can't compete with manually. Here's exactly what they're doing." Then walk through all 5 features as "what they're doing." Close with: "And this is only a fraction of what you can do on this platform. Everything I just showed you — $9 a month. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** This is the brief played pretty straight. The gold rush metaphor is strong, the main statement is well-written, and you just let the feature demos do the heavy lifting. Simple, urgent, effective.
**Duration:** 60s

---

## 2. "The Irrelevance Warning" — Fear-Forward, Then Save

**Brief element:** Hook Option #6 + FOMO #6 + FOMO #7
**Hook:** "Everything you've been doing so far as a creator will be irrelevant in 6 months."
**Structure:** Hit them with the bold claim, then weave in FOMO: "The gap between creators using AI and creators not using AI is getting wider every single day — and it's about to become permanent." Then pivot: "But the good news? I'm about to show you the exact platform that closes that gap." Walk through all 5 features. Land with FOMO #7: "Six months from now, AI-produced content will be the baseline. If you're still learning it then, you're not early — you're late." CTA: "$9 a month. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** Strongest fear-based open from the brief. The "irrelevant" hook creates a pit-in-your-stomach feeling, then you rescue them with the solution. The double FOMO lines at the end create real urgency to act now.
**Duration:** 60-90s

---

## 3. "How to Become Irrelevant" — The Reverse Psychology Play

**Brief element:** Hook Option #2 + FOMO #2
**Hook:** "The fastest way to become irrelevant as a creator — explained in 60 seconds."
**Structure:** Pause for a beat after the hook. Then the reveal: "Ignore AI while everyone else figures it out." Hit them with FOMO #2: "The creators who figure out AI right now are going to be untouchable in 12 months. The ones who don't are going to wonder what happened." Then: "So let me show you what the smart ones are using." Walk through all 5 features fast — this is a 60-second format so spend ~8 seconds per feature. End with: "This is an entire production studio, not a single tool. $9 a month. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** The reverse psychology hook is the most clever one in the brief. People click expecting a mistake to avoid, get hit with "it's you ignoring AI," and now they HAVE to keep watching to not be that person. The 60-second time constraint in the hook itself creates a built-in pacing promise.
**Duration:** 60s

## 4. "Hundreds of Millions of Views" — Pure Social Proof

**Brief element:** Hook Option #3 + FOMO #1 + References (creator names)
**Hook:** "This tool landed creators hundreds of millions of views this year."
**Structure:** Name-drop the reference creators from the brief: "Creators like John Bucog, Cleo Abram, Sam Kolder — they're way ahead of you because they're already integrating AI into their production workflows." Then: "And they're all using platforms like this one." Walk through all 5 features, showing YOUR generations. After the walkthrough: "And I just showed you five things. There are dozens more features on this platform. This is an entire production studio, not a single tool." CTA: "$9 a month. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** Leading with real creator names and a massive view count claim is impossible to scroll past. The brief specifically says to mention these creators by name or show their content as examples — this idea leans into that fully.
**Duration:** 60-90s

Then: "Let me show you the multiplier." Walk through each feature and frame each one as "one piece of a bigger system" (exactly what the brief says). After all 5: "And this is only a fraction of what you can do on this platform." CTA: "Everything I just walked you through — $9 a month. The earlier you start experimenting, the further ahead you'll be. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** The "half your talent, twice your output" FOMO line is probably the most visceral one in the entire brief. It hits creators right in the ego. Combined with the "here's what they know" hook, this creates a strong "I need to know this NOW" urgency.
**Duration:** 60s

Then pivot to hope: "But here's the thing — catching up is easier than you think." Walk through all 5 features. After the demos, hit FOMO #4: "The people who learned Reels early in 2020 built empires off it. This is that moment again — except this time, the window is closing faster." CTA: "$9 a month. AI is moving fast. The earlier you start experimenting, the further ahead you'll be. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** This stacks THREE of the brief's FOMO lines together, which creates an almost overwhelming sense of urgency. But you balance it with "catching up is easier than you think" so it doesn't feel hopeless — it feels actionable.

## 7. "A Photographer, a Studio, and Hours of Editing — From a Single Prompt"

**Brief element:** Feature 1 framing + Feature 2 framing + Feature 3 framing (the brief's own "land the point" lines)
**Hook:** "What if I told you this photo wasn't taken by a photographer, lit by a studio, or edited in Photoshop?"
**Structure:** Open with a stunning AI-generated image of yourself. Hook them with the reveal. Then walk through HOW: "This is a photographer, a studio, and hours of editing — from a single prompt" (Soul 2.0). "You never need to reshoot. Every image you have is now raw material" (Relight). "You don't need Photoshop skills. You just describe what you want. Anyone can art-direct now" (Nano Banana). "I just went from a still image to a scroll-stopping video in seconds" (Kling 3.0). "My entire feed stays visually consistent with no color grading skills" (Soul HEX). End with: "This is an entire production studio, not a single tool. $9 a month. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** This idea literally uses the brief's own "land the point" copy for each feature as the script. Those lines are already well-written and punchy — you're just stringing them together with a visual-first hook. Minimal adaptation needed, maximum brief alignment.
**Duration:** 60s

## 8. "The 2020 Reels Moment Is Happening Again" — Historical Parallel

**Brief element:** FOMO #4 + Main Statement + Hook Option #1
**Hook:** "The people who learned Reels early in 2020 built empires off it. This is that moment again — except this time, the window is closing faster."
**Structure:** Use the FOMO line AS the hook (it's strong enough to stand alone). Then deliver the main statement: "There's a gold rush happening in the creator economy. The creators who see it are producing studio-level content faster, cheaper, and at a scale you can't compete with manually." Walk through all 5 features. After the demos: "And I just showed you five things. There are dozens more." CTA: "AI is moving fast. $9 a month. The earlier you start experimenting, the further ahead you'll be. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** The 2020 Reels comparison is powerful because a lot of your audience LIVED that — they either caught the wave or missed it and regret it. This taps into that regret and says "don't miss it twice." The brief buried this as a FOMO line but it's honestly strong enough to be the entire hook.
**Duration:** 60s

## 9. "Studio-Level Content, Faster, Cheaper, At Scale" — The Speed Demo

**Brief element:** Main Statement (key phrase) + Feature walkthrough as rapid-fire demo
**Hook:** "What if you could produce studio-level content faster, cheaper, and at a scale that no human team could match?"
**Structure:** Pull the key phrase from the main statement and turn it into a question. Then: "Let me show you. Not a concept — I'm doing it right now on screen." Rapid-fire walkthrough: generate a pro image (Soul 2.0), relight it instantly (Relight), edit it with one sentence (Nano Banana), animate it into video (Kling 3.0), match the colors to your brand (Soul HEX). Keep pacing FAST — the brief says "content tip format, not a tutorial." After all 5: "Five features. One platform. An entire production studio. $9 a month. Link in bio."
**Why this angle:** This strips everything down to pure speed and demonstration. No storytelling, no FOMO layering — just "watch me do this impossibly fast thing." The brief says keep energy high and pacing fast, and this idea takes that to the extreme.
**Duration:** 45-60s

**Structure:** Adapted from FOMO #2. Let it sit for a second. Then: "The gap between creators using AI and creators not using AI is getting wider every single day — and it's about to become permanent" (FOMO #6). "So let me show you what the first group is using." Walk through all 5 features. After the demos: "And this is only a fraction of what you can do on this platform." CTA (straight from the brief's direction): "Everything I just walked you through — $9 a month. Link to Higgsfield is in my bio. AI is moving fast. The earlier you start experimenting, the further ahead you'll be."
**Why this angle:** This frames it as an identity choice — which type of creator do you want to be? The brief's FOMO #2 and #6 are arguably the two strongest urgency lines in the entire document, and stacking them back-to-back creates a "oh shit, I need to act" moment before you even show the product.
**Duration:** 60s
