# Script 1 — "Every Post Feels Like Starting Over"

**60s | Viral | DM Keyword: SYSTEM**

---

If every single piece of content you make feels like you're starting an entirely new Instagram from scratch — this is for you.

And no, you don't need to be more disciplined. You don't need to wake up earlier. You don't need to "just be consistent."

You need a system.

Here's what most people do — they wake up, they open Instagram, they scroll for 20 minutes trying to get "inspired," then they sit there for an hour trying to think of an idea, then they try to write a script, then they film it 47 times, then they give up and say "I'll post tomorrow."

Here's what I do. I sit down with a hook library — that's just a list of proven hooks that already went viral. I pick one. I plug it into a script template that has the structure already built out — hook, build-up, value, CTA. I write the script in 5 minutes. I film it in one take because I'm just reading and paraphrasing. Done.

Same system every single day. That's why I can post daily without burning out. It's not motivation — it's a machine.

DM me the word "SYSTEM" and I'll send you the exact content workflow I use so you never stare at a blank screen again.

---

**Caption:** Every post feels like starting over? You don't have a discipline problem. You have a system problem. DM me "SYSTEM" for the workflow.

**Filming Notes:**
- Talking head, direct to camera, high energy
- Hand gestures when describing "the old way" vs "the new way"
- Could split-screen or cut between "struggling creator" vs "you with the system"
- Keep it raw — no fancy edits, just you talking fast and real

# Script 2 — "I Got Scammed By a Coaching Program"

**90s | Conversion | DM Keyword: REAL**

---

I spent over $30,000 on coaching programs and courses — and most of them were complete garbage.

And I know a lot of you have had the same experience because I just got off the phone with like 9 different people this week and almost every single one of them told me the same story.

So let me tell you exactly what to look for so you never get burned again.

Red flag number one — they sell you "1-on-1 coaching" but when you get inside, it's a group Zoom with 50 other people and 3 of them get attention. The coach isn't even on the calls — it's some random assistant. That's not coaching. That's a lecture hall.

Red flag number two — the course is like 47 hours of pre-recorded videos with no roadmap, no timeline, no "do this first, then this." Just a giant dump of information that makes you MORE overwhelmed than when you started.

Red flag number three — you DM the coach with a question and they take 3 days to respond. Bro, you paid four, five, six thousand dollars and this person can't text you back? Nah.

Red flag number four — the "guarantee" has 47 stipulations. "Results guaranteed*" — asterisk, you have to post 900 times, attend every call, do a backflip on a Tuesday. That's not a guarantee. That's a legal escape hatch.

Here's what a real program should look like — personalized roadmap for YOUR business. Direct access to the actual coach. Fast response times. Real accountability. And a guarantee that actually means something, like "if you don't get results, I'll keep working with you for free until you do."

I'm telling you this because I got burned so many times before I figured it out, and I don't want you making the same mistakes I did. The right mentor will change your life. The wrong one will just take your money.

If you've been burned before and you're skeptical about investing in yourself again — I get it. DM me the word "REAL" and let's just talk about where you're at. No pitch.

---

**Caption:** I spent $30K+ on courses and programs. Most were trash. Here are the 4 red flags I wish I knew before I gave anyone a dollar. DM me "REAL" if you've been burned too.

**Filming Notes:**
- Raw and personal — sit down, look into camera, no hype energy
- Slow down for the red flags, let them land
- Get slightly heated when describing the bad experiences — your audience will FEEL it because they've lived it
- End with genuine empathy, not a pitch

So here's why most of them fail and what to look for instead.

The typical group coaching model is designed to make the COACH rich, not the student successful. Here's how it works — they sell you "personalized mentorship" for $3K, $5K, $7K. You get inside and it's a Zoom call with 50 other people.

The coach talks for an hour. Maybe 3 people get to ask questions. You leave more confused than when you joined. You DM the coach and they hit you back 3 days later with a voice note that has nothing to do with your actual situation.

Direct access to the coach — not an assistant, not a community manager, the actual coach. And real accountability — like someone checking in on you daily, not just "post in the community when you need help."

Oh, and a guarantee that doesn't have 47 asterisks next to it. If your coach isn't willing to keep working with you until you get results, they don't believe in their own program.

I built my program the opposite way because I LIVED through the bad ones. I know what it feels like to hand someone $5K and get nothing back. So I just made the thing I wish existed when I was starting out.

DM me "MENTORSHIP" if you want to know what a real coaching program looks like before you invest in anything. I'll tell you exactly what to look for — whether you join mine or someone else's.

---

**Caption:** Most group coaching programs are designed to make the coach rich, not the student successful. Here's how to tell the difference. DM me "MENTORSHIP" before you invest in anything.

**Filming Notes:**
- Start calm, get progressively more heated — this is a rant, let it build

# General Filming Tips

- Talking head, direct to camera for all scripts
- Natural lighting, clean background — authority through setting
- Paraphrase the scripts, don't read word-for-word — bring YOUR energy
- Simple edits — jump cuts, text overlays for key phrases
- Captions on every reel (accessibility + silent scrollers)
- Post between 8-10 AM or 6-8 PM for your audience
