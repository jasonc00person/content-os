**Youtube Anatomy**

Before diving into Youtube, you must first understand how the platform works. Youtube is a very eclectic platform in the sense that it attracts people from all industries, countries and backgrounds.

For this reason, there are no specific constraints on how you can communicate. The way you communicate will be much more dependent on your audience. Nevertheless, here are the 4 key elements you must know before you start creating content on Youtube.

1. Video Idea

Any video you make should start with a big idea.

2. Title

Together with your thumbnail, your title is what will get your audience to click on your video.

3. Thumbnail

Together with your title, your thumbnail is what will get people to want to watch your video. Think about how you can make your visuals aesthetically enticing.

4. Video

Your video must align and deliver on the promise of your title and thumbnail. When creating Youtube videos, put yourself in your audience's shoes and consider these three things:

- What the viewer must do
- What the viewer must feel
- What the viewer must know

The general structure of Youtube videos looks something like this:

**Hook → Story → CTA**

- Hook: Make a bold claim or ask an intriguing question
- Story: Share story and provide value
- CTA: Direct viewers to take action

Youtube Golden Rules

Below you will find a full list of what to do and what to avoid on Youtube:

→ Satisfy your title and thumbnail immediately

→ Deliver value immediately

→ Do not have a welcome or intro segment.

→ Do not overly talk about your channel.

→ Do not ask people to like and subscribe.

→ Build tension with your points.

→ Keep the outro simple with a CTA, if applicable.

**Content Strategy**

YouTube is the best platform when it comes to building a qualified audience.

Why?

It's long-form nature allows people to watch your content for hours, which strengthens your connection with your audience. The 7-11-4 rule states that for someone to go from a viewer to a buyer they must first watch your content for 7 hours, across 11 touchpoints and 4 different locations. This is exactly what YouTube allows you to do.

When it comes to Youtube strategy, always have the 3 C's in mind: content, context and consistency. Just by following this, you will already be ahead of 90% of creators.

1. Content: High-quality videos that document your journey, opens people's eyes or educates.

2. Context: Understand your audience's needs and desires and deliver the right message at the right time.

3. Consistency: Follow a regular and predictable posting schedule that your audience can count on. Long-form Youtube content takes more time to create, sso make sure to keep this in mind. You can find more information inside the

Content Planner

.

Post Optimization

On Youtube, consider the key elements below:

Thumbnails & Titles → Your video's first impression matters more than ever. Create eye-catching thumbnails and pair them with titles that spark curiosity.

Hook → Hook viewers in the first 15 seconds. Analytics show that losing viewers at the start of your video means potentially losing them forever.

Quality → Good audio matters more than perfect video.

SEO →Use relevant tags and descriptions to help your audience find your videos.

CTA → All your videos should have an intention. Be specific with what you want viewers to do next and don't ask for likes, comments and subscriptions all at once. Pick one clear action and make it clear to them.

Now let's go into the different types of Youtube content you can create.

You can post long-form videos or Youtube Shorts.

Long-form videos are the most common type and are better for building a qualified audience. These are a great way to increase the session watch time of your audience, which is a key metric when it comes to Youtube performance.

Shorts are easier to create and are great for delivering quick value. They have higher potential for virality and can be used to drive traffic to your channel and long-form videos.

For Youtube Shorts, here is the structure to follow, as well as what works:

**Hook → Story → CTA**

- Hook (first 3 seconds): Make a bold claim or ask an intriguing question
- Story (next 20-30 seconds): Share key insight or tutorial
- CTA (last 5-7 seconds): Direct viewers to full video or lead-magnet

Shorts Best Practices:

1. Optimal Length: 30-40 seconds performs best for retention
2. Title Strategy: Less than 5 words to avoid truncation
3. Posting Frequency: 1-2 shorts per day is optimal
4. Audio: Use trending sounds where relevant
5. Vertical Format: Film in 9:16 aspect ratio
6. Pattern Interrupts: Change scenes every 2-3 seconds
7. Face-to-Camera: Maintain eye contact for engagement
8. Strong Contrast: Use bold text and clear visuals

Avoid:

1. Hashtags (limits reach)
2. Repetitive account mentions
3. Long video attachments
4. Generic welcomes or intros
5. Complex explanations
6. Low-quality audio
7. Horizontal footage
8. Cluttered visuals

**Sales & Growth**

Now, let's go over how you can optimise Youtube for sales and growth.

Let's use Ryan Deiss as an example. Ryan is a business owner who has used Youtube long-form videos to grow his personal brand. Despite his 20+ years of business success and his impressive portfolio, he understood one simple truth:

**A personal brand is the most profitable asset on the planet.**

Interestingly, when compared to big creators like Mr Beast, Ryan does not have such a sizeable Youtube audience.

Despite only having 27,000 subscribers, Ryan Deiss has built a highly profitable YouTube channel by focusing on quality over quantity. Rather than chasing views, he:

- Creates content specifically for high-level business owners
- Shares actionable systems from his $200M company
- Focuses on actionable content over entertainment
- Uses YouTube to sell his high-ticket product
- Maintains high engagement despite lower subscriber count

The result?

His channel attracts highly-qualified viewers and turns them into customers at a significantly higher rate than many other creators with bigger audiences.

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-12-07%20at%2013.42.59.png)

Thus, when using Youtube to build your personal brand, focus on being intentional and attracting the right audience. Vanity metrics like views and subscribers don't matter if the people you attract are unqualified.

There are people out there who want to follow you and buy what you sell. You just have to go out there and find them. The best part about it is, if you package your videos using the packaging structures below, you don't need to chase them - they will come to you.

# Thumbnail Breakdowns

Below are some thumbnail A/B tests from Iman's channels, along with a breakdown of why certain thumbnails outperformed others. This will help you understand how to design thumbnails that get your audience to click on your videos.

Breakdown 1

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-11%20at%2017.40.49.png)

The original thumbnail showed a basic sit-down shot accompanied by icons representing seven productivity hacks. In contrast, the variation captured Iman actively journaling with a calendar schedule overlay, demonstrating organizational skills in action.

This shift from static to dynamic presentation proved more effective. The variation succeeded by showing a more relatable and aspirational moment that viewers want to achieve in their own lives. In turn, this makes them more likely to click compared to the original's more basic, icon-based approach. Therefore, mid-action thumbnails tend to outperform static presentations by creating a stronger emotional connection with the audience.

Breakdown 2

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.54.45.png)

The variation thumbnail outperformed the original, despite the original's dramatic presentation. While dramatic thumbnails often perform well, the variation connected more effectively by showing four app icons that viewers struggling with digital addiction could instantly relate to. Iman's frustrated facial expression captured the emotion of someone wanting to break free from these apps - a feeling that resonates with viewers facing similar challenges. The variation also used brighter, more vibrant colors that helped it stand out in YouTube's feed, compared to the original's darker tones.

Additionally, the clear visual elements ensured everything remained legible even on mobile devices, demonstrating how proper contrast and visibility of thumbnail elements directly impacts performance.

Breakdown 3

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.54.56.png)

The original thumbnail proved more successful by incorporating three highly relatable elements for the target demographic: a porn addiction reference, a game controller referencing wasted time, and "4:12 AM" representing poor sleep habits. The visual execution strengthens the message through balanced colors and Iman's deliberately exhausted expression, which reinforces the "messed up life" narrative suggested by the title.

In contrast, the variation thumbnail failed to achieve the same impact due to the weaker relatability. This example reinforces the golden rule of thumbnail design:

Create content that resonates with your audience's pain points and experiences.

Breakdown 4

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.04.png)

This comparison highlights an interesting case where both thumbnails associate Iman with success, but with a crucial difference in presentation. The original shows Iman in an upscale setting, positioned high above the city to represent elite status. However, the variation outperformed it by capturing Iman mid-conversation, drink and cigar in hand, in a similar high-end environment.

The key difference was the dynamic, mid-action shot that made the content feel more authentic and conversational. The variation succeeded because it presented Iman as both aspirational and approachable - a successful figure actively sharing wisdom. His appearance, charismatic demeanor and natural pose resonated with viewers seeking mentorship from someone living their desired lifestyle.

Breakdown 5

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.13.png)

Q&A videos are mostly about questions and expressions. As we can see, the original thumbnail has multiple questions. However, notice how Iman and Amara’s expressions do not align with the title “Answering Uncomfortable Questions With My Personal Assistant”.

On the other hand, the variation thumbnail focused on the one question that matches the title perfectly. Moreover, the expressions are on point. On the original thumbnail there’s a lot going on, which is not ideal. The recommendation is to stick to 3 elements. In this case, Iman, Amara and the question.

Breakdown 6

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.21.png)

This comparison demonstrates why realistic before and after visuals outperform abstract representations. While both thumbnails used this same concept, the variation succeeded through strong real-life visuals that viewers could instantly understand.

The original's small progress bars and icons lacked impact and realism. The variation's straightforward approach combined relatability with visual simplicity, making the transformation message immediately clear to viewers.

Breakdown 7

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.32.png)

This breakdown shows why a clear transformation narrative succeeds. The variation uses a direct before and after comparison of young Iman versus current Iman, with ages clearly marked, making the progression instantly clear.

The original's single image of young Iman lacked impact since viewers might not recognize him in that. More importantly, this demonstrates how investing time to find perfect thumbnail images is just as crucial as the ideation and execution. Before and after comparisons are extremely effective.

Breakdown 8

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.42.png)

In this comparison, the variation succeeded by combining two powerful elements: transformation and relatability. While the original thumbnail isn't poorly executed, it focus on money (showing a graph and specific amount) misaligns with the video's core message about habits.

This demonstrates how thumbnail elements must directly support the video's primary topic rather than introducing unrelated themes, even if they're eye-catching.

Breakdown 9

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.50.png)

This breakdown illustrates the effectiveness of controversial A vs B comparisons in thumbnails. By positioning Chat GPT (a widely recognized AI chatbot) against an alternative tool for wealth creation, the thumbnail creates intrigue through controversy.

This approach combines the proven before and after format with strategic controversy, challenging viewers' assumptions about a popular tool, while also providing a better solution.

Breakdown 10

![](https://sweetp-user-uploads.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/stage/39036/pages/975752/Screenshot%202024-11-17%20at%2017.55.59.png)

This comparison demonstrates how subtle elements can dramatically impact viewer engagement. The original shows Iman giving ice cream to children - a nice moment but lacking intrigue. The variation succeeded through multiple compelling elements. The stark contrast between a wrecked van, the African setting and the "Millionaire" title creating an intriguing juxtaposition.

Additionally, the variation captures Iman in motion, crossing a busy street, making the "Day In The Life" aspect feel more authentic and dynamic. The combination of movement, visual contrasts and the unexpected context create a more compelling reason to click.
