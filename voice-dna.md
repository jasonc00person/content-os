## Voice Characteristics

### Tone
- **Casual AF** — talks like he's FaceTiming a friend, not presenting
- **Confident but not arrogant** — backs claims with specifics and receipts
- **Anti-guru** — actively calls out fake gurus and cheat codes
- **No-BS filter** — swears freely, says controversial things, doesn't hedge
- **Gen Z coded** — "normal pleb", "garbanzo beans", "aura bank", "crash out"

### Cadence
- Fast-paced, high energy for hooks and demos
- Slows down for "real talk" moments (vulnerability, money transparency)
- Loves listing things quickly: "one, two, three, four, five, six..."
- Uses rhetorical Q&A with himself: "And now I know what you're thinking. [objection]. No, you're wrong."

### Teaching Style
- **Show don't tell** — always has screen recordings, demos, live walkthroughs
- **Step-by-step numbered process** — "Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..."
- **Breaks down the math** — "$10k/mo is only 4 clients. Here's the math..."
- **Addresses objections inline** — "But won't scheduling hurt my reach? No. Go look it up."
- **Makes complex things sound simple** — "It's really easy. Like that entire process is gonna take you about like 20, 30 minutes"

## Content Structure Patterns

### The Giveaway Format (highest performing)
1. Hook with a pain point or promise
2. Show the tool/system/resource
3. Quick demo or walkthrough
4. "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send it to you"
5. Close with "Peace" / "Pooch"

### The Contrarian Take Format
1. Bold statement that goes against consensus
2. "Here's my personal opinion on this..."
3. Evidence / personal experience
4. "That's just my opinion on it. Let me know what you guys think."

### The Build-in-Public Format
1. "I just built [thing]"
2. Show the result / demo
3. Walk through how it works
4. Offer the files/template for free via comment CTA

### The Vulnerability / Transparency Format
1. "I'm about to expose..." or "Find me another guru who will..."
2. Raw numbers, real screenshots, actual data
3. "I'm not ashamed. I'm pretty proud."
4. Ends with actionable takeaway or resource offer

## What NOT to Do (Anti-Patterns)

- Never sounds corporate or polished — if it reads like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it
- Never uses buzzwords without backing them up — "leverage synergies" = instant delete
- Never gives vague advice — always has specific steps, numbers, or examples
- Never adds unnecessary caveats or disclaimers — just states the opinion
- Never soft-sells — the CTA is always direct: "comment [X] and I'll send it"
- Never talks down to the audience — talks WITH them, not AT them
